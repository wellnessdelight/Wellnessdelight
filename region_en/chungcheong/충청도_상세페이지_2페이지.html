#nature
Uirim Reservoir
Uirim Reservoir is an artificial reservoir that supplies agricultural water to land. It has been a reservoir for more than 1,000 years and has continued its function until recently. Along the embankment of Uirim Reservoir, old trees such as willow and pine trees are abundant, and old pine trees are shaded on the flatland walkway, making it a good place to stay in the summer to cool off. Yongchu Falls Glass Observatory, a new specialty in Uirimji Reservoir in 2020, has its surprise fun, the iron pillar made up of opaque glass turns into transparent glass and can see waterfall. More and more people are visiting this place to see the charm of Uirimji, including the newly created Solbat Park and the Nooweora Garden, a resting place for citizens.

#healing/meditation
Palm Camille
Palm Camille is a romantic green space that sublimates the emotions felt by the five senses into art. Palm Camille started as a herb farm in May 2004 and has now became a wellness theme park with 100,000 people visiting. In Palm Camille, you can enjoy gardening, herb workshops, aromatherapy experiences, healing cafes, bakeries, and mud experience program located 5 minutes away from the Little Prince Pension inside the farm.

Forest Resom
Forest Resom, where you can enjoy the beautiful four seasons of dense primeval forests, is an eco-friendly resort developed in a way that does not damage nature. Forest Resources is designed to preserve natural terrain and vegetation and use eco-friendly materials to relax with nature. Therefore, you can't find electric wires and cars in the complex. You can only meet beautiful wildflowers, squirrels, and blue squirrels all year round. This resort also offers exhibitions at the Wellness Studio ‘Muyougonggan’ located in the facility, or night-time shooting of constellations at the cozy Igloo Dome.
Homepage : https://www.resom.co.kr/forest/main/main.asp
Address : 365, Geumbong-ro, Baegun-myeon, Jecheon-si, Chungcheongbuk-do
Tel : 1600-0060
#oriental_medicine
Oriental Medicine Life Science Museum
In Oriental Medicine Life Science Museum, you can learn about our bodies, the history of diseases, the principles, diagnosis, and treatment of oriental medicine through 4D images and various oriental medicine experiences. Located in Jecheon Oriental Medicine Expo Park, Oriental Medicine Life Science Museum is free of charge, and you can experience hop therapy and wellness hop picnic programs using the eco-friendly beer raw material hops grown in Jecheon for the first time in Korea. Hop Therapy/Picnic is a wellness program for young women or lovers interested in hop beauty, spa, and is an easy-to-experience wellness program for beginners.
