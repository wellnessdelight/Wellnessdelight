Korea Wellness Industry Association

The Korea Wellness Industry Association planned the vision of by fostering the wellness industry. It aims to create profits for members who will lead the 4th industrial revolution centered on people and to build a win-win cooperation platform between the public and private sectors. Therefore, the association is carrying out the following projects to protect rights and interests and develop industries. (see below)

1. Domestic and international exhibitions and participation support projects for member promotion and marketing
2. Certification and education projects developING member products and trainING OF professionals
3. Friendly business for the protection of rights and interests and strengthening cooperation among members
4. Government cooperation projects to improve the policies, laws and systems of the wellness industry
5. Publication of a wellness journal to identify trends in wellness related to rural convergence industries and tourism and to provide information
66. Construction of a wellness town (tentative name) to support the start-up and commercialization of the domestic wellness industry
7. Other projects necessary to achieve the purpose of this corporation

These projects by the Korea Wellness Industry Association helps K-Wellness industry to strengthen the identity and create jobs.

Korea Wellness Tourism Festa

The Wellness Tourism Festa was the 1st Wellness Tourism Festa held in Korea. It was from November 6th to 8th, under the leadership of the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism, with the theme of travel and healing. Prior to the full-fledged resumption of international tourism, it was designed to preemptively attract wellness tourists to Korea and provide valuable travel opportunities for health and healing to domestic and foreigners.

During the Wellness Tour Festa, 36 different places were recommended as a healing (wellness) tourist attractions nationwide. These wellness attractions provided unique opportunities such as cypress forest barefoot experience and natural salt healing programs, with te discount up to 30,000 won for 6,000 Koreans and foreigners. They also ran an online real-time experience event for tourists from China, Southeast Asia, and Russia who are difficult to visit Korea. In addition, five Middle Eastern influencers who focuses on Korean healing (wellness) tourism were invited to explore major tourist attractions in Seoul/Incheon/Gangwon.

The Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism expects the Wellness Tourism Festa to be a precious memory for people who got tired of Covid pandemic. They are looking forward to spread Korean wellness tourism as an opportunity to make a leap forward in the sluggish tourism industry.

Wellness Tourism Cooperation Zone (Cluster) Project in 2022

'The Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism' is working on the project to create new demand for the tourism industry by discovering and fostering unique healing tourism resources in regions in Korea. They selected and supported Gyeongsangnam-do in 2018, Chungcheongbuk-do in 2019, and Gangwon-do in 2020. In 2022, they continued to support them by comprehensively evaluating the performance of the '21 year project and the '22 year project plan'.

This year, Gyeongsangbuk-do (Yeongju, Yeongyang, Yeongdeok, Bonghwa, and Uljin) was designated as the "Wellness Tourism Preliminary Cooperation Zone" in 2021. In addition, along with the Korea Tourism Organization (President Ahn Young-bae), 50 different places were recommended as healing (wellness) tourist destinations in 2019. By strengthening strategic support for healing (wellness) tourism, 'the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism' is looking forward to the growth of the tourism industry.

'The Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism' expects the discovery of healing tourism resources by region will contribute to the growth of local tourism.
