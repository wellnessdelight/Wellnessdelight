Jogyesa Temple
Jogyesa Temple was built by the monks wishing for the independence of Joseon and the restoration of national pride. Jogyesa Temple has a good accessibility. Foreign tourists who want to experience Buddhist and Korean culture must visit. It has accommodations right in front of Daeungjeon Hall where temple stay program is held. They have three different temple stay programs: 'resting', 'experiencing', and 'one-day'.
Homepage : http://jogyesa.templestay.com
Adress : 55 Ujungguk-ro, Jongno-gu, Seoul
Tel : 02-768-8600

The Season of Tea
The Season of Tea is a tea cafe with a comfortable atmosphere where you can enjoy seasonal refreshments selected by the tea master. The tea course runs only three days from Thursday to Saturday, and only one team receives reservations by the hour. Which means you can enjoy the food slowly while listening to 1:1 customized explanations in a private space. In addition to tea courses in the tea season, you can also enjoy afternoon tea sets made with different fruits and tea from season to season.
Homepage : https://korean.visitkorea.or.kr/detail/ms_detail.do?cotid=e56eb20c-87dd-47c6-81c7-680331bf2f9f
Address : 170, Gyeryong-ro 342beon-gil, Seo-gu, Daejeon
Tel : 042-487-1596

Hapcheon Odosan Forest of Healing
Odosan Forest of Healing is the best place to enjoy forest healing. It is known for its impressive view of the pine tree forest. You can experience various healing programs, such as silver yoga, aromatherapy, and lower-body baths that help the recovery of the body from fatigue. Breathing fresh air while walking along the street and sitting in a shelter for meditation will make you relieve stress and feel relaxed.
Homepage : https://www.foresttrip.go.kr/indvz/main.do?hmpgId=ID02030068#
Adress : 208 Odosan Recreational Road, Bongsan-myeon, Hapcheon-gun, Gyeongsangnam-do
Tel : 055-930-3733
