Daegwallyeong National Center for Forest Therapy
It is one of the seven healing forests run by the Korea Forest Service; and Daegwallyeong National Center for Forest Therapy is specialized in its Geumgang pine trees. You can choose the right level from 8 different trails. Because the longest trail is just 1.6km, everyone can walk up the course easily. Relax your body and mind with fresh air from the Geumgangsong Observatory, and don't forget to take a glance at Daegwallyeong from the top. If you make a reservation in advance, you can also experience various forest healing programs.
Homepage : https://www.fowi.or.kr/user/contents/contentsView.do?cntntsId=140
Adress : 127-42, Daegwallyeong Old Street, Gangneung-si, Gangwon-do
Tel : 033-642-8651

Daegu Arboretum
Daegu Arboretum is an arboretum built on landfills to solve environmental problems. It consists of gardens of 25 different themes. Because it is an urban arboretum located close to the city center, it makes it convenient to visit even on a short trip. Also, take a look around the mountain nearby and Duryu Park for an amazing view.
Homepage : http://www.daegu.go.kr/Forestry
Address : 342, Hwaam-ro, Dalseo-gu, Daegu
Tel : 053-803-7270

Daemyung Retarding Basin & Dalseong Wetland
Daemyung retarding basin is an artificial reservoir created in 1995 to prevent flooding of the Nakdonggang River. It is 260,000㎡ sized reservoir filled with pampas grass, known to be a fascinating photo spot. You can also see millions of birds in autumn. Dalseong Wetland is a large wetland with an area of 2 million square meters. There are many endangered species living there. In the past, access to wetlands was prohibited, but it recently opened after the completion of the ecological restoration project. You can experience wellness tourism through the forest paths created by ginkgo, maple, cherry, and zelkova trees.
Homepage : https://dalseo.daegu.kr/tour/index.do
Address : 816 Daecheon-dong, Dalseo-gu, Daegu
Tel : 053-667-2171

Manisan Forest of Healing
Manisan is the highest mountain on Ganghwa Island. Manisan Mountain's Forest of Healing, designated as a 'UNTACT' tour attraction in Korea, has a 1 km short hiking course. While walking along the forest path full of pine and cedar trees, there are shelters ready for the you to take a minute to meditate and enjoy the fresh breeze.
Homepage : http://www.ganghwafocus.com/exp/index.php
Adress : 18, Manisan-ro 675beon-gil, Hwado-myeon, Ganghwa-gun, Incheon
Tel : 070-4101-8308
