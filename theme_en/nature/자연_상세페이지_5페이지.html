Oil Tank Culture Park
Oil Tank Culture Park is an oil reserve base that is a legacy of the industrial era. This place, where they had strictly controlled access and use of the general public for 41 years, has been reborn as an ecological and cultural park through urban regeneration. You can rent an audio guide (Korean, English, Japanese, Chinese) from the information center for further information. Don't forget to check out the cultural events such as exhibitions, performances, and workshops held at the Oil Tank Culture Park.
Homepage : http://parks.seoul.go.kr/culturetank
Adress : 18-11 Seoul Forest 2-gil, Seongdong-gu, Seoul
Tel : 02-376-8410

Boryeong Mugunghwa Arboretum
Boryeong Mugunghwa Arboretum is located at the foot of Seongju Mountain, called the lungs of Boryeong, and is known for the best wellness attraction where you can enjoy clean air and various plants. Boryeong Mugunghwa Arboretum was created as a story-themed arboretum by collecting, proliferating, preserving, managing, and exhibiting tree genetic resources centered on Mugunghwa, and securing research infrastructure for its resource conversion. In addition, pear blossom and bridal wreath with 170m long white tunnel, which bloom from April to May every year, is popular as the best photo spots.
Homepage : http://www.mugunghwapark.com/
Address : 318-57, Seongjusan-ro, Seongju-myeon, Boryeong-si, Chungcheongnam-do
Tel : 041-931-6092, 041-930-4097

Village Full of Oxygen and Happiness
You can refresh while breathing fresh air walking through the Chukryeong forest. It is a space realizing well-being and happiness through various forest-related programs and various experiences that can only be experienced here. There are Chukryeong Forest Experience School for Students, Rural Experience School, and Chukryeong Amusement School, which are special programs for the village. It offers a new tour called studying in the countryside.
